## 8. Interpretation for Paper

### Finding: Open thematic space

Across 60 original transcripts, 2108 unique thematic codes were identified. The cumulative theme curve followed a power trajectory (R-squared = 0.992), consistent with a decelerating but non-asymptotic growth pattern.

The power-law exponent of 0.892 (near unity) indicates near-linear theme accumulation, meaning practical saturation is not foreseeable within feasible sample sizes. This suggests the synthetic persona pool and questionnaire together produce a highly generative thematic space.

The refinement round (50 transcripts with the improved V4_R1 instrument) introduced 1817 additional themes (46.3% novelty), confirming that the thematic space was not exhausted and that instrument refinement can unlock new experiential dimensions even when the original curve suggests diminishing returns.

This pattern is consistent with qualitative research literature suggesting that thematic saturation is instrument-dependent: changing the instrument can reopen the thematic space (Hennink et al., 2017; Saunders et al., 2018).
