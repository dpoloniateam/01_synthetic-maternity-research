# Paper Brief for JPIM Submission

## Title
AI-Enabled Questionnaire Design for Maternity-Care Innovation: A Synthetic User Approach Combining Composite Personas and Electronic Health Records at the Front End of Innovation

## Target Journal
Journal of Product Innovation Management (JPIM)

## Theoretical Framework
Knowledge-Based View (KBV) of the firm (Grant, 1996; Nonaka, 1994)
Dynamic capabilities / micro-sensing (Teece, 2007; Day, 1994)
AI in innovation management (Gama & Magistretti, 2025)

## Research Questions
RQ1: How can AI-enabled synthetic user research be used to design, stress-test, and comparatively evaluate multiple questionnaire versions for maternity-care user research?
RQ2: Which questionnaire design strategy produces the richest user knowledge in terms of breadth, depth, latent dimension surfacing, and innovation-relevant service gap identification?
RQ3: What configurations of resources, routines, and governance mechanisms constitute this capability, and what evidence of saturation and robustness validates its methodological adequacy?

## Key Contributions
1. THEORETICAL: Extends AI-for-innovation literature by operationalising composite synthetic personas as a knowledge-creation micro-capability at the front end of innovation.
2. METHODOLOGICAL: Introduces a replicable pipeline for multi-version questionnaire generation, BIBD comparative evaluation, and automated saturation/robustness analysis.
3. MANAGERIAL: Identifies specific service gaps and innovation opportunities in maternity care.

## Empirical Context
Maternity care across preconception, pregnancy, birth, and postpartum. 150 composite personas, 5 questionnaire versions, 355 interviews, iterative refinement, saturation analysis, and adversarial robustness testing.

## AI Disclosure (JPIM requirement)
Generative AI tools (Claude, GPT, Gemini) were used to generate synthetic maternity personas, conduct synthetic interviews, and score response quality. All AI-generated content is explicitly labelled as synthetic. AI tools are NOT listed as authors. Human researchers maintained oversight of all design decisions, theoretical framing, and interpretive analysis.
